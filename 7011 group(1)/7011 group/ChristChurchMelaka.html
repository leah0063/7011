Christ Church Melaka

Christ Church

Christ Church Melaka, located in the heart of Melaka's Dutch Square, is a prominent and iconic historical church. Recognized for its distinctive red facade, the church is a symbol of Melaka's colonial past and a significant heritage site.

Historic city

Location

Situated in Dutch Square, Christ Church Melaka is centrally located within the historic city of Melaka. It stands adjacent to the Stadthuys and other historical landmarks.

Architecture

The church features a simple yet elegant Dutch colonial architectural style. The exterior is characterized by red bricks, and the building has a rectangular plan with a pitched roof.

Construction

Christ Church Melaka was constructed in the 18th century. The building materials, including the red bricks, were shipped from the Netherlands.

Color Symbolism

The distinctive red color of the church is symbolic of Dutch colonial architecture. It has become one of the most recognizable landmarks in Melaka.

Stained Glass Windows

The church is adorned with beautiful stained glass windows depicting biblical scenes. The interior is relatively austere, emphasizing the simplicity of Protestant church design.

Interior Feature

Inside the church, visitors will find wooden pews, a simple pulpit, and a prominent pipe organ. The church's interior reflects the Protestant tradition.

Church Bell

Christ Church is equipped with a large church bell that was manufactured in 1698. The bell is an integral part of the church's heritage.

Historical Significance

The church served as a place of worship for the Dutch community during the Dutch occupation of Melaka. It is one of the oldest functioning Protestant churches in Malaysia.
