Food&Drink

Introduction

Malacca is not only a historical town, it is also an undisputed culinary destination. People spend their weekends on culinary excursions, and such trips often become a luxury that fills the stomach and stretches the belt. From seafood chicken rice balls to sweet and refreshing polenta, they can be found on the streets of Malacca, making this town the ultimate buffet of Malaysian cuisine.

Speciality Cuisine

Malacca Chicken Rice

Malacca chicken rice is a famous specialty food in Malacca. It is different from traditional chicken rice. Usually, chicken rice balls are rolled into small balls or ovals with rice, rather than served directly like traditional chicken rice. These little rice balls are usually slightly thicker than traditional rice and are served with tender white-cut chicken and some special sauce. This special chicken rice has a unique taste and is different from traditional chicken rice in flavor and texture. It is a classic delicacy in Malacca.

Nyonya cuisine

Nyonya cuisine is famous for its unique taste and flavor. Nyonya cuisine is a unique Chinese cuisine in Malacca, Malaysia, which combines Malay culture and traditional Chinese cooking techniques. Nyonya cuisine is named after Nyonya, which refers to the female descendants of intermarriages between Chinese immigrants and Malay aborigines. The classic specialty dishes of Nyonya cuisine include laksa, otak, Nyonya rice dumpling, Nyonya cake, black fruit braised chicken, etc.

Malacca’s Red Rice Bak Kut Teh

Malacca’s red rice bak kut teh is a delicacy full of local characteristics. It usually uses pork ribs, which are cooked and then marinated in a special red lees sauce. This red lees sauce is made from fermented rice koji, red yeast rice and a variety of spices, giving the ribs a unique red appearance and rich flavor. The cooked ribs are slowly marinated in the red lees sauce, absorbing the aroma and taste of the sauce. The result is a dish that is rich in the aroma of the sauce, and the ribs are tender and delicious, with a slightly sweet and slightly sour taste.

Satay Celup

Satay Celup (Malay: Satey Celup) is a food originally originated from the Teochew people in Malacca, Malaysia. In Malay, satey means satay, and celup means soaking. Therefore, satay skewers are made by skewering seafood, meat or vegetables on bamboo skewers. When you want to eat, put the skewers of ingredients into the hot sauce. Delicious food cooked with dadjiang sauce.

Taste of Malacca: Easy steps to make your own cendol shaved ice

Chinchalok shaved ice is a special delicacy in Malacca. It combines traditional Chinchalok with shaved ice to form a unique eating experience. This shaved ice features cendol, a shrimp paste made from fermented shrimp. When making cendol shaved ice, cendol is usually sprinkled on top of the shaved ice, making this dessert not only refreshing and relieving the heat but also has a slightly salty taste. The shaved ice has a smooth and refreshing texture, while the addition of cendol adds a unique twist to the overall taste. This dessert, which combines cold and slightly salty taste, is a unique snack in Malacca, giving people a refreshing taste experience.

Ingredients

  • Mung bean powder 22g
  • Corn starch 15g
  • Banlan juice 260g
  • Alkaline water 5g
  • Salt 0.5g
  • Coconut sugar 70g
  • Any amount of honey red beans
  • A bowl of shaved ice
  • Favorite fruit

Production Steps

  1. Boil coconut syrup: Boil 70 grams of coconut sugar cubes and 30 grams of water, set aside to cool.
  2. Add 150 grams of fresh variegated leaves to 300 grams of water and use a wall-breaking machine to extract the juice. After filtering, weigh out 260 grams.
  3. Add alkaline water (can be mixed with baking soda powder and boiled water at a ratio of 1:1), salt, mung bean flour and corn starch into the Banlan juice, stir evenly and then filter again.
  4. Pour the filtered mixture into the pot, stir constantly over medium-low heat until it becomes a translucent paste, then turn off the heat. Lower the temperature slightly and put it into a piping bag.
  5. Take out the pre-chilled boiling water from the refrigerator, cut a small opening in the piping bag, and squeeze the cendol paste into the ice water. Squeeze and set aside for later use.
  6. Take out the frozen milk and hit the milk carton with a rolling pin until it turns into crushed ice. Cut the opening and pour it into a bowl. The shaved ice is ready. Arrange the cendol strips, honey red beans, and durian (any fruit you like) in order, and finally pour coconut syrup, and you're done.
